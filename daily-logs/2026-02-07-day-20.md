## üß† Key Technical Insights

### 1. Shift From Code Risk to Decision Risk

Agentic AI systems introduce **behavioral risk**, not just software defects.

Unlike traditional RPA:
- Agents reason autonomously
- Decisions propagate across systems
- Failures are policy violations, not exceptions

This reframes governance as an **operating boundary**, not a compliance checkbox.

---

### 2. Enterprise Governance Problems Addressed by UiPath

Studied how the UiPath Platform mitigates:
- Unauthorized LLM data training
- Cross-region data residency violations
- Privilege sprawl in autonomous agents
- PII leakage during inference
- Lack of auditability in AI-driven decisions

---

### 3. AI Trust Layer (Core Platform Control)

All LLM interactions are mediated by the **AI Trust Layer**, which:
- Removes identifying metadata
- Masks PII before inference
- Rehydrates responses post-inference
- Logs every interaction for audit

This enforces **zero-trust AI usage** at scale.

---

### 4. Layered Governance Architecture

UiPath governance operates across three enforced layers:

**Agentic Governance**
- Controlled agency boundaries
- Behavioral guardrails
- Human approval for deployment changes

**IT Governance**
- Identity-bound agents
- Role-based access control
- Unified auditing for users, robots, and agents

**Infrastructure Governance**
- Encryption and network isolation
- Data residency enforcement
- Compliance with GDPR, ISO 27001, FedRAMP

This provides a full accountability chain from decision to data.

---

### 5. New Governance Capabilities (2025.10)

Conceptually reviewed:
- Agentic guardrails
- In-flight PII masking
- Bring Your Own LLM (BYO-LLM)
- Agent design policies (confidence, token limits)
- Unified audit in Automation Cloud
- ISO/IEC 42001:2023 certification
- Regional cloud expansion (Switzerland, UAE)

These convert governance from documentation into **configurable enforcement**.
