## 🔍 Problem Observation

Observed consistent failure in background playback during track transitions.

- Current track completes
- Next track does not autoplay
- No visible crash or UI error

### Key Finding

Locking the app in Recents prevents the issue completely.

### Initial Hypothesis

The issue may be caused by aggressive OEM background management (HyperOS) interfering with app lifecycle during track transition.

---

## 🧪 Debugging Process

Used targeted log capture to isolate failure moment.

### Method

- Cleared log buffer using:
  ```adb logcat -c```
- Triggered failure (track ending in background)
- Dumped logs immediately:
  ```adb logcat -d```

### Key Log Signals

- Playback reaches STOPPED / PAUSED state
- Foreground service notification is cancelled
- System reports interaction with "frozen apps" (binder errors)

### Interpretation

- The foreground service appears to be stopped immediately after playback ends.
- This creates a short window where the app is no longer protected as a foreground process.
- During this gap, the system freezes the app, preventing execution of next-track logic.

## 🧠 Key Learnings

- Background execution issues may originate from OS-level policies, not app logic
- Foreground service lifecycle is critical for uninterrupted media playback
- OEM-specific optimizations (HyperOS) can override standard Android behavior
